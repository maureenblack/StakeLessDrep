DRep Code of Conduct

This Code of Conduct outlines our expectations for all DReps (Delegated Representatives) within our community. We are committed to providing a welcoming and inclusive environment for everyone, regardless of background, identity, or beliefs. By participating as a DRep, you agree to abide by this code.

Expected Behavior

  • Be Respectful: Treat all community members with respect and courtesy. Disagreement is fine, but personal attacks, harassment, or discrimination will not be tolerated.
  • Be Inclusive: Foster an inclusive environment by welcoming diverse perspectives and experiences. Actively listen to and consider the viewpoints of others.
  • Be Responsible: Take responsibility for your words and actions. If you make a mistake, apologize and learn from it.
  • Be Empathetic: Show empathy towards others. Understand that people may be coming from different backgrounds and experiences.
  • Be Collaborative: Work collaboratively with other DReps and community members to achieve common goals.

Unacceptable Behavior

  • Harassment, discrimination, or any form of offensive behavior.
  • Personal attacks, insults, or threats.
  • Spreading misinformation or engaging in deceptive practices.
  • Disruptive behavior that interferes with community discussions or activities.
  • Any violation of privacy, including sharing personal information without consent.

Enforcement

Violations of this Code of Conduct may result in consequences, including warnings, temporary suspension, or permanent removal from the DRep program. Enforcement decisions will be made by the DRep governance team, based on the severity and context of the violation.

Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it to the DRep governance team at [insert contact email or reporting process]. All reports will be treated with confidentiality and respect.

Join Us

We invite all community members to join us in upholding this Code of Conduct and creating a positive and welcoming environment for everyone. Together, we can build a strong and inclusive DRep community.
